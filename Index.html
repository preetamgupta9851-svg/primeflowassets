What is lease-based financing?
We procure the asset and lease it to the end-user against predictable monthly rentals. Collections are structured through an escrow mechanism and monitored with disciplined risk controls—helping align growth, safety, and transparency.

Our Business Model

A structured end-to-end cycle: procure, deploy, collect, monitor, and transfer—built for transparency and governance.

1

Client request

Clients share a detailed list of equipment to be procured along with deployment requirements.

2

Asset procurement

PrimeFlow purchases the equipment on behalf of the client and formalizes the finance lease agreement.

3

Deployment & use

Assets are installed at the client site and monetized immediately through business operations.

4

Escrow-backed collections

Monthly lease rentals are collected through escrow for tighter repayment discipline and safety.

5

Monitoring & risk control

Regular inspections and contract penalties for misuse, damage, or deviation from agreed usage.

6

Ownership transfer

Upon lease completion, ownership transfers to the client at a nominal value as per agreement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions. Share your specifics and we will tailor the structure accordingly.

Who owns and maintains the asset?▾
Assets are owned by PrimeFlow and deployed at client sites. The client is responsible for maintenance.
How are returns distributed?▾
Investors receive monthly EMI payouts (net of expenses) from the first month. There is an option to reinvest via SIP in mutual funds.
Are returns guaranteed?▾
Returns are based on stable lease flows and escrow arrangements, but remain projections, not guarantees.
What are the exit terms?▾
There is a 3-year lock-in period. The amount is returned as EMIs over this tenure.
Who is eligible to invest?▾
Eligible investors include Individuals, HUFs, LLPs, Private Companies, Partnerships, Trusts, and NRIs.
